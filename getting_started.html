Overview

Our approach to image-based plant phenotyping is based on a distributed sensing and analysis framework. Image acquisition relies on an affordable and versatile imaging device (based on the Raspberry Pi single-board computer), that can be easily deployed for experiments with rosette plants in growth chambers or greenhouses. Using data compression and the Wi-Fi capabilities of the Phenotiki device, the acquired images can be easily downloaded to a local workstation. Optionally, the images can also be transmitted over the Internet to the BisQue platform running on the iPlant Collaborative cyberinfrastructure, offering cloud-based storage for life sciences research. The image data can be explored and analyzed on a local workstation using our standalone software, or on the iPlant cloud via web-based application, both providing an easy-to-use graphical user interface to perform sophisticated computer vision tasks. Our analysis system allows to delineate plant objects in the images and measure growth-related plant features (e.g., area, rosette diameter, relative growth rate, …). Phenotyping results are presented to the user for interpretation and can be exported for further statistical analysis.

Steps to affordable and easy phenotyping

We are updating detailed documentation, and preparing download packages and demos of our plant phenotyping system. Currently we have beta testers throughout the world, if you would like to deploy the system please contact us.

1. Obtaining the Phenotiki device

Obtain hardware components of the Phenotiki device the imaging device based on the Raspberry Pi. The equipment can be easily purchased from Farnell element14, which sells and ships worldwide. Afterwards, the Phenotiki device can be assembled following our detailed instructions.

2. Configure device for time-lapse acquisition

Connect the Phenotiki device to the Internet and configure time-lapse acquisition from its web-based interface (e.g., using a smartphone). Images will be stored on the device or automatically transmitted to the BisQue platform on the iPlant cloud.

Detailed instructions to setup the Phenotiki device are available in the user manual. The web-based interface to configure and operate the device from a browser is available for download as a ZIP archive and also as a GitHub repository.

3. Launch time-lapse acquisition and obtain image data

Start acquisition and let the device operate autonomously for the duration of the time-lapse. Upon completion, download images from the device to a local computer or browse them on the web-based BisQue platform.

4. Analyze image data

Analyze the acquired image data using the Phenotiki software on a workstation (standalone Matlab software) or on the cloud via web-based applications running on BisQue. The Phenotiki analysis software for image-based phenotyping of rosette plants is available in two versions: standalone and web-based.

5. Obtain phenotyping results

Visualize phenotyping results (plots) or export to spreadsheets for further analysis.
